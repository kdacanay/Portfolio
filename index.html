About me

Hi! My name is Ken Dacanay, and I am a 2nd generation Filipino-American. My parents immigrated here to the Philadelphia area in the 1970s, where they had established themselves and raised a close-knit and loving family. My mother is a registered nurse and my father was a certified accountant.

They both were very involved and beloved in the Filipino-American community. My father passed away in 2017 from leukemia, but before he left this earth, he had built a successful business and career. He and my mother worked so incredibly hard to give the best life possible for their 4 boys, all while dealing with the blending cultures they were raised with and their sons were surrounded with.

But it was my father's relentless drive to support his family and my mother's undying love that kept us together, and made me who I am today. I am a proud husband of over 10 years and a newly proud father to my daughter, Lily. I have delved into many careers over the years, from customer service to musician to graphic design to freelance writing, all of which I found myself obsessed with perfecting my craft. I have found a new love; web development, and I found myself coding several hours into the night, mostly because of my love of web development, but also because I am currently disabled with a rare autoimmune disease, which enables me plenty of time to learn new techniques and better myself.

And it is my family, my wife and my daughter, that drive me to be the best full-stack developer I believe I can be. Like the lesson I learned from my father, there may be others more talented or smarter than me, but no one will outwork me.
